Data Quality Summary:

Objective:
Evaluate dataset completeness, consistency, uniqueness, and suitability for analytics.

Key Findings:

1. Dataset Completeness
   • fund_master contains 40 schemes with no missing values.
   • nav_history contains 46,000 NAV records with no missing values.
   • No critical missing values were identified in key identifier fields such as AMFI code, scheme name, category, fund house, or NAV.
   • Attributes are descriptive and business-friendly, making their purpose easy to understand and naming conventions consistently indicate measurement units, such as:
    1. aum_lakh_crore
    2. sip_inflow_crore
    3. new_sip_accounts_lakh
    4. expense_ratio_pct
   
2. Data Consistency
   • AMFI codes are consistently maintained across fund_master and nav_history.
   • Categories, sub-categories, and risk categories follow standardized naming conventions.
   • No inconsistencies were observed in key identifier fields.

3. Data Uniqueness
   • No duplicate records were identified in fund_master.
   • No duplicate records were identified in nav_history.
   • In rest of the CSV files there were no duplicates found.
   • AMFI scheme codes are unique across the master dataset.

4. Data Type Assessment
   • Financial metrics such as NAV, expense ratio, and investment thresholds are stored in appropriate numeric formats.
   • launch_date record within 01_fund_master CSV file and date 03_aum_by_fund_house CSV file are currently stored as strings and should be converted to datetime format for time-series analysis.

5. Missing Value Assessment
   • The only notable 12 missing values were observed in yoy_growth_pct within monthly_sip_inflows.
   • These missing values appear business-valid because year-over-year growth cannot be calculated for the initial reporting periods.

6. Business Readiness
   • The datasets are sufficiently clean for exploratory analysis, dashboard development, trend analysis, and performance benchmarking.
   • No critical data quality issues were identified that would impact analytical outcomes.

Overall Findings:
The mutual fund datasets demonstrate high data quality, strong referential integrity, and consistent identifier usage. The data is suitable for further analysis, visualization, and business intelligence reporting.
